Why This Matters

The decisions AI changes — what to build, what to kill, where to grow — are yours. They require your judgment, your discernment, your knowledge of the business. You cannot delegate them to people who don't carry that context. And you cannot lead a transformation you don't understand with your own hands.

This course puts your hands on the wheel. Not theory. Not a vendor demo. You build a working AI system on your own computer, with your own files, aimed at your own work — and you walk away knowing what's possible because you've done it.

All 8 Prompts

Copy-paste ready. In order. Each one builds on the last.

① Create Workspace

Create a new folder on my Desktop called Claude-Work. Inside it, create four subfolders: - ABOUT ME - PROJECTS - TEMPLATES - OUTPUTS Tell me when it's done.

② Organize Your Files

Drop 5-10 real work files into PROJECTS first.
Look at everything in my PROJECTS folder. Organize the files: - Group related files into subfolders by project or topic - Rename anything that's unclear - Give me a summary of what you found and how you organized it

③ Generate About Me

Based on everything you just read in my PROJECTS folder, write an About Me file about me. Include: - Who I am and what I do - Who my audience or stakeholders are - What I'm working on right now - How I like to communicate - What success looks like in my work Write it like you're briefing a new colleague on day one — not a resume, a working briefing. Save it to my ABOUT ME folder as about-me.md. Then tell me: what did you get wrong? What are you uncertain about? I'll correct you.

④ Global Instructions

Paste into Settings → Cowork → Global Instructions.
# GLOBAL INSTRUCTIONS ## BEFORE EVERY TASK 1. Read my ABOUT ME folder first. No task starts without understanding who I am. 2. If the task relates to a project, read everything in the matching PROJECTS subfolder before proceeding. 3. If the task involves a format that has a matching template in TEMPLATES, study that template's structure first. ## FOLDER RULES - ABOUT ME, TEMPLATES, PROJECTS → read-only. Never create, edit, or delete anything here. - OUTPUTS → the only place you create files. One subfolder per project. ## HOW TO WORK - If the task is unclear, use AskUserQuestion before starting. Don't guess. - Don't over-explain. Deliver the work. - Never delete files anywhere.

⑤ The One Prompt — Real Work

Replace the brackets with your actual task.
I want to [DESCRIBE YOUR TASK] for [DESCRIBE WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE]. First, explore my Claude-Work folder. Then, ask me questions using the AskUserQuestion tool. I want to refine the approach with you before you execute.

⑥ Extract a Skill

Look at the work you just produced in my OUTPUTS folder. Extract the approach you used into a reusable skill file. A skill is a markdown file with: - A name (e.g., "meeting-synthesis" or "proposal-draft") - A description that tells Claude WHEN to use this skill — what trigger phrases or task types should activate it - Step-by-step instructions for HOW to do the work — same quality, same format, every time - Rules for what to do and what NOT to do Save the skill file to my TEMPLATES folder. Then explain what you built.

⑦ Use Connectors — Meeting Prep

Check my Google Calendar for the rest of this week. For each meeting: - Tell me who's attending - Look at any recent emails from those people in my Gmail - Draft a 3-bullet prep note: what's the meeting about, what do they likely need from me, and one question I should ask Save the prep notes to my OUTPUTS folder.

⑧ Scheduled Task — Monday Briefing

Create a scheduled task: Every Monday at 7:00 AM, do the following: 1. Check my Google Calendar for the week ahead 2. Check my Gmail for any unread messages from the past 3 days 3. Look at what's changed in my PROJECTS folder since last week 4. Write a Monday Morning Briefing that includes: this week's meetings, who I'm meeting with, anything that needs attention from email, and what's active in my projects Save it to my OUTPUTS folder as a new file each week.
