Art of the Possible

Context engineering for senior leaders. Five layers that turn AI from a chat tool into a compound intelligence system that knows who you are, how you work, and what you're building.

Why This Matters

Everyone is selling AI as a productivity tool. Write emails faster. Summarize meetings. It works. It's also a trap.

If all you use AI for is doing the same work faster, you're polishing a machine that might not need to exist. So much of knowledge work is work-shaped work — the meetings, the formatting, the coordination overhead that exists because humans are slow. AI doesn't just speed that up. In some cases it eliminates the need for it entirely.

The interesting question isn't "how do I do my job faster?" It's what becomes possible when the job itself changes?

Your expertise is the most valuable input in the system. A graphic designer with twenty years of vocabulary says "add a CRT scanline texture at 15% opacity" and gets exactly what they want. You say "make it look vintage" and get garbage. Same tool. Same model. The variable is precision. AI makes production cheap. The value of expertise holds. We just have to know how to deploy it.

That's what this course teaches. Not prompt engineering — context engineering. You give AI a persistent understanding of who you are, what you're working on, and how you think. You set it once. Every conversation starts from that foundation. And the system compounds — like a garden where compost feeds soil, soil feeds plants, and nothing goes to waste.
